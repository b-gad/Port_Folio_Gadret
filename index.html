Who I am

For twenty-one years, my daily life has been defined by "us." Growing up with a twin brother means having a constant mirror; it means being born into a team.

This relationship made one thing clear: ambitious projects come to life through the synergy of complementary talents. It also taught me healthy competition, pushing me early on to stand out through strong discipline.

For twelve years, the piano was my foundation. On the rugby field, I learned how to carry a team. In Lourdes, I understood that technical skill only matters if it serves others.

Today, my brother and I follow the same path in two different countries. I carry with me the legacy of this life for two: a standard of excellence forged in competition and a sense of collective purpose that has become instinctive.
